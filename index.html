Dixie Castle

Dixie Castle is a locally owned steakhouse in Jackson, Tennessee. The location of the first Dixie Castle is in Downtown Jackson, and owner Mark Hudgens has added two more locations. The other locations are on Bells Highway and Airways Boulevard. The first Dixie Castle was opened in 1937 across from the Jackson Sun. At this time Dixie Castle was known for their five cent burgers. In Dixie’s early years, they also served hot tamales and minute steaks. The current location was opened in 1985, and soon after bought by Mark Hudgens in 1989. Hudgens is the current owner of Dixie Castle, and has around 10 employees distributed across the three locations. Dixie Castle II was opened in 2004 and Dixie Castle on Airways was opened in 2014. Both of these locations were to expand Dixie Castle and hopefully draw in new customers. Although Dixie is known as one of the busiest locally owned restaurants in Jackson, COVID-19 has done a toll on business for Hudgens. That doesn’t stop Mark as he continues to make and sell incredible steaks, porkchops, grilled chicken, filets, and more.
